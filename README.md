# ARENA Curriculum

My implementations and notes as I work through the [ARENA (Alignment Research
Engineer Accelerator)](https://www.arena.education/) curriculum. This is an intensive
ML engineering program for AI safety research. ARENA's materials are openly
available for self-study; this repository tracks my own worked solutions and
experiments.

I'm working through ARENA to build hands-on ML engineering depth, implementing
fundamentals from scratch rather than working only with pretrained models
and APIs to round out my experience and support my work in AI safety and research engineering.

## Curriculum structure

The curriculum is organized into four core chapters, followed by
alignment-science material and a capstone project. Progress tracked below:

- [ ] **Chapter 0 — Fundamentals** *(in progress)* — PyTorch, tensor
  manipulation, building and training neural networks (CNNs, ResNets),
  optimization, and backpropagation.
- [ ] **Chapter 1 — Transformer Interpretability** — building and training a
  transformer; mechanistic interpretability, circuit analysis, and sparse
  autoencoders.
- [ ] **Chapter 2 — Reinforcement Learning** — RL fundamentals, DQN, PPO, and
  RLHF.
- [ ] **Chapter 3 — LLM Evaluations** — building benchmarks and evaluations
  from scratch; LM agents.
- [ ] **Capstone / paper replication**
